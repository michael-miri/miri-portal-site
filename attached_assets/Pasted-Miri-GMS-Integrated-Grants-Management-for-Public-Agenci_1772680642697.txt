Miri GMS — Integrated Grants Management for Public Agencies

Miri GMS is a purpose-built grants management platform designed for port authorities and public-sector organizations managing complex federal, state, and local grant portfolios. Built by Miri Technology, it provides end-to-end lifecycle management from application through closeout — all in one unified system.

Full Grant Lifecycle Management
Track every grant from initial application through award, post-award administration, and final closeout. Manage budgets, milestones, drawdown requests, and compliance requirements at each stage with built-in status tracking and lifecycle progression.

Multi-Entity Support with Strict Isolation
Manage grants across multiple organizational entities — such as the Port of Tacoma and the Northwest Seaport Alliance — from a single platform. Role-based access control ensures that users only see and interact with data they're authorized to access, with a global entity filter for instant cross-entity or entity-specific views.

Comprehensive Reporting & Analytics
Seven dedicated reporting views — Portfolio, Financial, Budget Utilization, Milestones, Drawdowns, Compliance/Audit, and Entity Comparison — give leadership and grant administrators the visibility they need. Every report supports CSV export for easy sharing and integration with existing workflows.

Complete Audit Trail
Every action is logged with full attribution — who changed what, when, and why. Detailed change history with before-and-after values supports 2 CFR Part 200 compliance and audit readiness out of the box.

Role-Based Access Control
Three user roles — Administrator, Manager, and Viewer — provide the right level of access for every team member. Administrators manage users, review audit trails, and configure the system. Managers handle day-to-day grant operations across entities. Viewers get read-only access scoped to their assigned entity.

Built for Compliance
Designed with federal grant compliance requirements in mind, Miri GMS supports the administrative standards of 2 CFR Part 200, including budget tracking, milestone documentation, drawdown management, and audit-ready record keeping.
